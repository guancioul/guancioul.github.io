My personal shortlist, grouped by interview type. Short notes on what each is good for.

- [Tech Interview Handbook](https://www.techinterviewhandbook.org/) — **start here**; free and covers the whole loop — coding, system design, behavioral, resume, negotiation, and more

## Tech interview

- [NTHU OCW — Operating Systems](https://ocw.nthu.edu.tw/ocw/index.php?page=course&cid=141) — Prof. Jerry Chou’s open course (Mandarin); covers processes, memory, synchronization, file systems, and more
- [KodeKloud](https://kodekloud.com/) — hands-on labs for Linux, Kubernetes, Docker, Terraform, and related infra topics; **worth the investment** — learn by doing, not just watching

## Coding interview

- [LeetCode](https://leetcode.com/) — problem bank and contests
- [NeetCode](https://neetcode.io/) — curated roadmaps and pattern groupings on top of LeetCode
- [Structy](https://www.structy.io/) — structured DSA course with guided problems; **best ROI in my book** — full walkthroughs for every problem, not just hints or final answers
- [AlgoMonster](https://algo.monster/) — patterns-first coding interview prep

## System design

- [System Design School](https://systemdesignschool.io/) — structured course + interactive practice with feedback
- [Design Gurus — Grokking the System Design Interview](https://www.designgurus.io/course/grokking-the-system-design-interview) — classic FAANG-style system design patterns
- [ByteByteGo — System Design Interview](https://bytebytego.com/courses/system-design-interview/) — full course (scale from zero to millions, rate limiter, URL shortener, chat, YouTube, etc.)

## Behavioral interview

- [Design Gurus — Grokking Modern Behavioral Interview](https://www.designgurus.io/course/grokking-behavioral-interview) — structured prep for behavioral and culture-fit rounds
- [Amazon Leadership Principles](https://www.amazon.jobs/content/en/our-workplace/leadership-principles) — LP definitions and videos; useful as a framework for STAR stories beyond Amazon too
